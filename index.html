    Data Analytics - Research & Reports

    Stakeholder Name Description Presentation
    Medical Tourism Association (MTA) 2013 MTA
    Medical
    Tourism
    Survey
    Report    		 The 2013 MTA Medical Tourism Survey Report aimed to fill information gaps in medical tourism, focusing on empirical data collection and analyzing demand drivers, popular treatments, economic impact, preferred destinations, and factors influencing tourists' choices. Research was based on a self-administered online survey questionnaire scheduled between April 8, 2013 and April 19, 2013.

    With the selection of 500 sampling units from a total population of 860 website registrants, the survey interview achieved nearly a 10% response rate. Key findings include cost savings as the primary demand driver, cosmetic surgery as the most sought-after treatment, and significant contributions to global GDP. The study highlights the United States as the largest market for medical tourism, with Mexico and India respectively being the top destination countries.
    Medical Tourism Association (MTA) 2015 MTA
    Medical
    Tourism
    Survey
    Report    		 The 2015 MTA Medical Tourism Survey focused on understanding the dynamics of medical tourism. It aimed to collect empirical data, analyze motivations and treatment demands, and explore economic impacts and choice factors in medical travel. Research was based on a self-administered online survey questionnaire scheduled between July 4, 2013 and August 10, 2013.

    With the selection of 1,650 sampling units from a total population of 2,300 website registrants, the survey interview achieved nearly a 9% response rate. The study found a strong future interest in medical tourism primarily driven by cost savings. Cosmetic, dental, and orthopedic treatments were most in demand. The top destination countries respectively were Mexico, the United States, and Costa Rica. The survey revealed that quality of care, cost, and physician expertise significantly influence destination and provider choices.
